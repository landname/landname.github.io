Urban Change from Historical Redlining Maps ASIS&T 2025

Community Informatics Lab · 2024 –
  • Quantifies long-run urban change by linking 1930s HOLC redlining boundaries with road-network change and census records across cities in Colorado, Ohio, and North Carolina.
  • Developed a map-anchored extraction pipeline that pulls road networks from scanned 1940s city maps using computer vision (thresholding, clustering, and ridge-detection methods) to recover roads that no longer exist.
  • Validated on Denver's Auraria neighborhood, recovering 7.2 km of demolished streets.
  • First-author short paper at ASIS&T 2025; full study in progress.
