Research Interest 01

Predicting workplace injury before it happens

With the Virginia Department of Labor and Industry, I build statistical and machine learning models on OSHA enforcement, Census, and geospatial data to estimate injury and fatality risk across Virginia's industries — so limited inspection resources go where they matter most. The hardest part, and the part I care about most, is the rare-event frontier: fatalities are statistically rare but human in cost.

Research Interest 02

Reading a century of urban change from old maps

The 1930s HOLC "redlining" maps encoded decisions that still shape American cities. I quantify what happened next — extracting road networks from 1940s scanned maps with computer vision, linking them to redlining grades and census records, and measuring how neighborhoods grew, changed, or were erased. This work appeared at ASIS&T 2025.

Research Interest 03

Making public-sector AI legible

Governments are adopting AI faster than anyone can track. I built an NLP pipeline that collects U.S. federal AI use cases from inventories and news, disambiguates them with text-similarity methods, and summarizes them with LLMs into a structured, browsable clearinghouse — so researchers and the public can actually see how government uses AI.
